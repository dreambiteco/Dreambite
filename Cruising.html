CRUISING

A sizzling, subversive romcom about polyamory, protest, and passion in a warming world.

Everyone tells lies when they’re trying to impress someone, right?

When Amelia, a sparky and confident dancer on a cruise ship, meets Sam, a quietly witty, alleged climate activist, they each twist the truth in a bid to impress. Their chemistry is undeniable and it seems like this will be the beginning of a perfect holiday romance...until they realise the other has been playing fast and loose with the truth, and they actually share a romantic partner back home. In an attempt to impress each other and their absent partner, Sam and Amelia embark on a risky campaign to sabotage the ship and raise awareness of its environmental impact. As Sam and Amelia become increasingly entangled in their activism and each other, the very real-world consequences of their actions close in. With the risks increasing, something is bound to explode – will it be the boat, their relationships, or their lives?

Cruising is a sexy exploration of civil disobedience which grapples with climate anxiety, polyamory, and personal sacrifice. It takes a queer, unconventional approach to traditional romcoms. In paralleling the collective nature of polyamory with the community of activism, Cruising asks its audience who can and should make sacrifices both within relationships and in the face of the climate crisis. The play’s warm, romcom narrative is a ‘Trojan horse’ for essential conversations about responsibility - pertinent in this political moment when the need to take action is urgent, yet our right to protest is becoming more and more restricted.

JOURNEY SO FAR

Contact us for the full rehearsed reading recording, or the scratch night extract!

CRUISING was first presented as part of En Route's Setting Sail Scratch Night at the Golden Goose Theatre, in October 2025. An extract of the play was presented.

It was then selected as part of Omnibus Theatre's Engine Room: Next Page, as part of their season of rehearsed readings, staged for a public audience.
